The Definition

Positioning is the place your offer occupies in the customer's mind compared to their alternatives.

The key phrase is compared to alternatives.

Customers Never Evaluate You Alone

You are always being
compared against:

Direct competitors

Doing it themselves

Hiring internally

Software or AI

Asking a friend

Watching YouTube

Their current vendor

Doing nothing

The Most Dangerous Competitor

Your biggest rival isn't another company. It's the customer doing nothing.

Because doing nothing is free, familiar, low-effort, low-risk emotionally, and easy to postpone.

First Principle · People Avoid Change

People buy only when staying the same feels more expensive than changing.

Why this is true

Every purchase is a trade: a certain cost now (money, effort, the risk of looking wrong) against an uncertain gain later. People overweight certain losses, so "do nothing" wins by default. Positioning's real job is to tip that scale, making the cost of not changing concrete and immediate.

✕ Cost of staying the same

A clinic loses 30% of slots to no-shows. That's ~RM8,000 a month in empty chairs, quietly, every month.

✓ Cost of changing

An automated reminder system: ~RM600 a month, live in a week.

Sell "reminders" and they shrug. Make the RM8,000 visible and RM600 becomes obvious. Same offer, different cost of inaction.

Section Three

ICP definition

Finding the buyer most likely to act, beyond demographics.

1 Position→2 ICP→3 Trigger→4 Stand out→5 Offer + Proof
First, what it actually means

ICP = Ideal Customer Profile

Ideal

Best-fit, not average

The customer you serve best, and who pays, stays, and refers.

Customer

A real buyer, a real situation

Not a vague market. A person with a problem, right now.

Profile

A repeatable pattern

The shared traits and triggers you can target again and again.

Why it isn't "everyone"

Your ICP is the narrow slice where pain, urgency, budget, and fit all line up. Serve them first and your ads, sales calls, and referrals all sharpen at once. Try to serve everyone and you serve no one well enough to be chosen.

How to find your ICP

Five questions. Watch Sigmaschool answer them.

Ask yourselfSigmaschool's answer
1. Who gets the best result from us?Career-switchers who actually ship projects, not casual hobbyists
2. What painful problem do they have?"AI is making my skills and my job feel worthless"
3. What triggered it right now?A layoff, a salary ceiling, or watching AI do their work
4. What can they pay and commit?Some savings, plus 12 weeks, and serious about switching
5. Can we reach and serve them well?Yes, via AI-and-jobs content, delivered remote-first
Demographics tell you who they are. These five tell you who will actually act.
ICP Is Not Demographics

Weak ICPs:

"SMEs"

"Working adults"

"Students"

"Founders"

"Women aged 25–40"

"People who want to improve"

Useful data points. Not enough to position on.
ICP Is a Buying Situation

A strong ICP includes:

  • 1 Who they are
  • 2 What situation they're in
  • 3 What pain they feel
  • 4 What triggered the pain
  • 5 What outcome they want
  • 6 What alternatives they're weighing
  • 7 What they can afford
  • 8 What they already believe
  • 9 What they need to believe
  • 10 The words they use for the problem
A situation, layered — not a label
The Best ICP Is Not the Biggest Audience

Your best ICP is the segment where pain, urgency, budget, reachability, and delivery fit overlap.

Narrow on purpose — the overlap is small

Then what · how to use the scores

Score every candidate segment 1 to 5 on all seven, then add up each segment's total. The highest total is your beachhead, the segment you win first. A low score doesn't mean a bad customer, just a harder place to start. Win the easy one, then expand from strength.

People Buy Because Something Changed

People don't buy because they fit your ICP. They buy because something created urgency.

Buying Triggers

Something shifts, then they look.

Revenue dropped

Leads slowed down

Competitor growing faster

New technology appears

Regulation changes

Team grows

Founder overwhelmed

Complaints increase

Vendor disappoints

Status anxiety rises

Big opportunity appears

Risk of inaction gets obvious

Example · Deel
✕ No trigger, no sale

A company with an all-local team never thinks about global payroll. Deel could be perfect and they'd still never look.

✓ Trigger fires, urgent buy

They hire their first engineer abroad. Suddenly: no legal entity, no compliant way to pay them, a start date looming.

Deel becomes urgent the day that offer is accepted. The remote-work boom handed Deel millions of these triggers.

Same product, same buyer. Nothing sells until something changes.
Example · Peloton
✕ No trigger, no sale

Someone who's "always been meaning to get fit." A RM10,000 bike feels absurd. They scroll past for years.

✓ Trigger fires, urgent buy

2020: their gym shuts down. The usual option vanishes overnight.

Millions bought the same expensive bike, not because it got cheaper, but because the trigger made staying still feel worse than buying.

The product didn't change. The world did, and that created the urgency.

The Proof Gap

The distance between what the buyer currently believes and what they must believe before buying.

Proof is what closes the distance
The Bigger the Ask, the Bigger the Proof Gap

Beliefs required to say yes

◦ Small purchase

Low cost, low risk, easy to undo. A coffee, a RM20 app, an impulse add-to-cart.

3

Beliefs needed: interest · convenience · basic trust

◆ High-ticket purchase

Big cost, high risk, hard to reverse. A RM15k bootcamp, a car, a year-long retainer.

7+

Beliefs needed: trust · the problem · the solution · themselves · timing · cost · risk of inaction

More than 2× the belief, and you can't manufacture it in one ad.
Example · The RM15k Bootcamp

Before they buy, they climb four beliefs.

1 · Why now

It can't wait

AI is reshaping every job. Standing still is the risky move, not learning.

2 · Why me

3 · Why tech / AI

The path is worth it

Coding isn't dead. AI-native skills are worth more, not less.

4 · Why Sigmaschool

This is the way

A proven, project-based path with a money-back job guarantee.

It's a journey. Each belief unlocks the next, and only the last one is about us. Most marketing jumps straight to step 4.
Strategic Principle

If the proof gap is too big, don't only improve your marketing. Create a lower-friction experience that helps the buyer prove the value to themselves.

The third word · Offer

An offer is a promise, not a product.

A strong offer makes four things clear

  • 1 The transformation, before and after
  • 2 Why now, the trigger
  • 3 Risk reversal, what removes their fear
  • 4 The terms, time, price, what's included
My offer · Sigmaschool

"In 90 days, go from AI-anxious beginner to shipping real software, with a money-back guarantee if you're not job-ready."

Transformation + why-now + risk reversal + terms, in one line.

Why the offer matters to positioning

Positioning decides who you're for and how you're seen. The offer is where that becomes a concrete promise they can say yes to. A vague offer forces the buyer to imagine the value; a sharp offer hands it to them, which is why a stronger offer shrinks the proof gap we just covered.

The Positioning Statement Formula
For specific ICP
who are struggling with pain / problem
triggered by why now
we provide offer / category
that helps them achieve outcome
unlike alternative
through unique mechanism / proof
Worked Example · Sigmo

Underlined parts are the slots you fill in.

For beginners curious about AI but unsure they can really build, Sigmo provides a 7-day AI Build Week that helps them ship their first AI-powered app, unlike passive coding courses, through missions, AI tutor support, debugging, explain-back, and public proof.

Worked Example · Notion (real company)

For teams whose docs, notes, and tasks are scattered across five apps, Notion provides one connected workspace that helps them keep everything in a single place, unlike juggling separate tools, through flexible building blocks anyone can assemble.

Worked Example · Agency

For founder-led service businesses relying too much on referrals, we provide a predictable growth system that helps them create steady inbound leads and follow-up, unlike generic digital marketing agencies, through offer repositioning, lead capture, content, and sales automation.

Worked Example · Fitness

For busy professionals who gained weight after starting office work, we provide a 12-week coaching system that helps them lose their first 5kg without extreme dieting, unlike generic gym plans, through simple meals, strength training, and weekly accountability.

Worked Example · HR / Hiring

For startups that just raised funding and need to scale fast, we provide technical hiring support that helps them hire their first 10 engineers, unlike traditional recruiters, through role scorecards, founder-led screening, and startup-specific pipelines.

The 10-Question Positioning Checklist
  • 1 Who exactly is this for?
  • 2 What situation are they in?
  • 3 What pain do they feel?
  • 4 What changed that makes it urgent?
  • 5 What alternatives are they considering?
  • 6 What must they believe before buying?
  • 7 What proof can we create before the sale?
  • 8 What mechanism makes us different?
  • 9 Who are we intentionally not for?
  • 10 What should the customer say after seeing us?
The ideal reaction: "This is exactly for me."
Final Takeaways
  • 1 If your message is broad, your conversion will suffer.
  • 2 ICP is about urgency, not just demographics.
  • 3 Positioning is comparison against alternatives.
  • 4 Differentiation requires tradeoffs.
  • 5 The higher the price, the bigger the proof gap.
  • 6 Your front-end offer should create belief before the main sale.
